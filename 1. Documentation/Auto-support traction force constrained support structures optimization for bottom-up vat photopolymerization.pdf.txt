    ABSTRACT                                                                                                                                     ARTICLE HISTORY
    Bottom-up vat photopolymerisation offers high precision and speed, but the process is influenced                                             Received 17 December 2024
    by separation forces, which vary with the cross-sectional area of printed layers—often overlooked in                                         Accepted 30 January 2025
    existing support structure generation methods. Insufficient supports lead to printing failures, while
                                                                                                                                                 KEYWORDS
    excessive supports require manual intervention and degrade surface quality. Optimising support                                               Support structures; vat
    structures to account for separation forces is challenging due to the computational expense of                                               photopolymerization;
    repeated simulations. This paper introduces a novel methodology to optimise support structures,                                              separation force; support
    minimising support usage while satisfying force and overhang constraints. The approach                                                       optimization
    combines representative layer simulation and geometric optimisation with simulation
    verification, reducing simulation iterations from hundreds of thousands to a few dozen. Layers
    are clustered by cross-sectional shape coherence, with representative layers showing the
    maximum support traction forces identified for full simulations. These simulations guide and
    validate geometric optimisation to meet both overhang and force constraints. Experimental
    results show that this approach significantly reduces support contact points compared to
    commercial software while maintaining structural stability throughout the printing process.
