# Support Types

## Purpose
This document defines the support structures used in the STL Slicer POC so we can refer to them consistently when planning new tooling, UI, and automation. The naming follows the tree analogy used across the project.

### Terminology Notes
- Use "contact cone" for the final segment at the model/support interface. Avoid using "tip" except in external tool mappings.
- The tree analogy (trunk/branch/leaf/twig) is conceptual. When precision matters, prefer the formal terms defined in Support Elements.

> Trunk vs Shaft (at a glance)
> - **Trunk**: the full chain of one or more shaft segments whose base is rooted to the build plate (via raft). It’s a whole unit from the base up toward the model.
> - **Shaft**: a single straight segment between two joints, or between the base and the contact cone. Multiple shafts make a trunk or a branch.

> Leaf vs Twig (at a glance)
> - **Leaf**: a minimal shaft with a contact face on the model end and a socket face on the support end (connects support → model).
> - **Twig**: a minimal shaft with a contact face on both ends (connects model → model; no socket faces, no other supports).

## Plain-Language Guide (What each thing is, simply)

- **Raft (starter pad)**
  - A flat, sacrificial pad printed first so everything sticks to the plate.
  - It grows just big enough to connect all supports that touch the plate—no extra waste.
  - Example: A white “pancake” under your supports so the print doesn’t fall off.

- **Trunk (main support)**
  - The chain of one or more shaft segments that starts on the raft/build plate and goes up toward the model.
  - Think: the main pole holding things up. Other supports can attach to it.
  - Example: One sturdy column under a large overhang.

- **Branch (support attached to a support)**
  - A full support that starts on a trunk instead of on the plate.
  - Same parts as a trunk; only its base snaps to another support.
  - Example: A side arm coming off the main pole to reach a nearby overhang.

- **Contact cone (tip piece)**
  - The small end that touches the model and the wider end that plugs into a support.
  - Purpose: touch the model gently but firmly so it’s easy to remove later.
  - Example: A tiny cone at the very end of a support where it meets the model.

- **Shaft (body)**
  - One straight piece between two joints (or between the base and the contact cone). Several shafts in a row make the trunk or a branch.
  - Thickness changes strength: thicker = stronger; thinner = lighter/easier cleanup.

- **Base (foot)**
  - The round/flat piece that the shaft stands on.
  - It either sits on the raft/plate (trunk) or clamps onto another support (branch/brace end).

- **Base Flare (cone foot)**
  - A cone that spreads out from the thin shaft to a wider circle on the build plate.
  - Makes supports stick better and less likely to fall off during printing.
  - Example: Like a tree trunk that widens at the ground—more stable than a thin pole.

- **Joint (hinge ball)**
  - A little ball along the shaft that lets you angle the support without moving where it starts or ends.
  - Example: Bend a branch around a shape without losing the target contact.

- **Leaf (small contact from support to model)**
  - A tiny, minimal shaft with a contact face on the model end and a socket face on the support end.
  - Use when you need multiple light touch points close together.
  - Example: One branch splits into a few small contacts on a curved surface.

- **Twig (tiny contact from model to model)**
  - A tiny, minimal shaft with a contact face on both ends—no socket faces; no other supports involved.
  - Use to tie thin areas together inside the model where a full support would scar the outside.

- **Brace (bar between trunks)**
  - A support that connects one trunk to another to stop wobble.
  - Think: a stabilizer bar so tall supports don’t shake during peeling.

## Tree Analogy
- **Support system** = a tree.
- **Trunk** = the full chain of main segments that starts on the build plate (rooted base) and runs up toward the model.
- **Branches** = additional full-featured supports that attach to an existing trunk instead of the build platform.
- **Leaves** = single contact-cone elements where the contact face touches the model and the socket face mates into a branch or trunk.
- **Twigs** = contact-cone-only supports where both faces of the cone contact the model directly (no other supports involved).
- **Roots** = the bases, rafts, or anchor pads that connect supports to the platform.

## Shared Anatomy
All support types share the same components, scaled to their role.

| Component | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Contact cone | Terminal segment that houses the two faces of a support: the **contact face** (small end touching the model) and the **socket face** (larger end that mates into a branch or trunk). Usually conical but can be cylindrical when both ends share a diameter. |
| Shaft (segment) | A single straight piece of a support between two joints (or between base/contact cone). Multiple shafts in a row form the trunk (if anchored at the plate) or a branch (if anchored to another support). |
| Base joint | Optional cone/cube element that transitions from shaft to base and can include articulation (ball joints) when enabled. |
| Base | Foot or pad securing the support to the platform or to another support. |
| Joints | Ball joints inserted along the shaft to articulate or re-aim a support without moving its contact cone or base. |

## Support Elements
These terms describe every physical piece we reference while developing support tooling.

### Contact Cone
- Final geometry segment at the end of a support.
- Contains two critical faces:
  - **Contact face**: the small end that penetrates or rests on the model surface.
  - **Socket face**: the larger end that mates into a shaft (branch/trunk/brace). It does not attach directly to the model.
- Face rules (always):
  - Contact face → model only.
  - Socket face → shaft only.
- Parameters cover contact/socket diameters, body diameter, length, penetration, and cone angle. Cylindrical variants occur when both faces share the same diameter.
- May include breakpoint logic for multi-stage cones but always terminates at the model surface or another support interface.

### Shaft / Mid Section
- A single segment of a support between two joints (or between base/contact cone).
- Carries load locally; usually cylindrical but can be cubed or tapered.
- Settings control diameter(s) and whether adjoining joints allow the overall path to bend.
- Trunk = one or more shaft segments whose base is rooted to the plate. Branch = one or more shaft segments whose base clamps to another support.

### Base Joint
- Transitional element between shaft and base that can include articulation (ball joint) or angled cone for smoother load transfer.
- Houses parameters such as contact diameter, body diameter, length, penetration, cone angle, and rotation allowances.

### Base / Foot
- The immediate contact pad for whatever the support is anchored to (plate, trunk, or branch socket).
- Includes diameter, height, neck dimensions, and blend factor to soften transitions.
- Bases on branches/brace ends clamp to other supports; trunks land bases on the build plate (or on the raft if present).

### Base Flare
- Optional conical transition element that extends from the build plate up to the support shaft for improved adhesion and stability.
- **Geometry**: Truncated cone with the narrow end meeting the shaft and the wide end spreading on the build plate.
- **Top Diameter**: Automatically matches shaft diameter - 0.1mm (creates smooth transition without overlap).
- **Bottom Diameter**: 3.0mm default (adjustable via `baseFlare.diameterMm`), provides wider footprint for better adhesion.
- **Height**: 1.5mm default (adjustable via `baseFlare.heightMm`), creates gradual taper angle.
- **Application**: Only renders on trunk supports (not branches), works with or without raft enabled.
- **Purpose**: Increases contact area with build plate, reduces support failure from poor adhesion, creates smoother transition from thin shaft to plate.
- **Settings**: Controlled via `SupportSettings.baseFlare` with `enabled`, `diameterMm`, and `heightMm` properties.

### Joints
- Ball joints inserted along shafts for articulation.
- Each joint has its own diameter, order, and optional movement limits and can be added or removed interactively.
- Moving a joint reaims every segment above it without changing tip/base locations.

### Raft (Sacrificial Base)
- Large sacrificial platform printed first on the build plate to guarantee adhesion and distribute peel forces.
- Dynamically shapes itself to encompass every support that touches the plate, connecting them into one continuous footprint while avoiding excess material.
- Includes chamfered edges, venting, and top-skin texture tailored to current support layout; deleting or adding trunks/branches updates the raft outline accordingly.

### Leaves
- Minimal contact-cone elements whose contact face touches the model while the socket face plugs into a branch or trunk.
- Provide terminal contact points without adding shaft length; ideal for distributing load from a branch to multiple nearby surfaces.
- Never touch the build plate directly and inherit transforms from their parent support.

### Twigs
- Single contact-cone supports that touch the model at both faces to create tiny model-to-model braces.
- Do not rely on other supports for anchoring; instead seat both faces into the model geometry.
- Useful for reinforcing thin model regions internally where adding a full support tree would scar exterior surfaces.

### Rooted Base
- The bottom-most base that anchors a support stack to the build plate (often via the raft).
- Any segment chain that terminates at a rooted base is considered part of the trunk.
- Removing or moving the rooted base affects every branch or leaf that ultimately depends on that trunk.

### Trunk Chain
- The complete sequence of shaft segments that run from the rooted base up to the highest joint before another support attaches.
- Includes every main-diameter segment directly aligned with the base, regardless of how many joints it passes through.
- Functionally identical to a branch in geometry; its defining trait is that its base connects to the build plate (directly or through the raft).

### Branch Support
- A full support (contact cone through base) whose base clamps to a trunk rather than the build plate.
- Can be discussed as an entire support or by its individual shaft segments; either way, it inherits the same geometry as a trunk chain.
- Converting a branch to a trunk (or vice versa) simply changes its base attachment target—geometry settings, joints, and leaves remain the same.

## Standard Support (Trunk)
This is the support type currently generated by the tool.

- **Role**: Primary vertical column that rises from the build platform (or raft) to a contact cone on the island.
- **Structure**:
  - Contact cone sized by `SupportSettings.tip`.
  - Straight shaft (`isTrunkStraight` true by default) using `SupportSettings.mid` diameters.
  - Optional joints (default count = 1) inserted to bend the trunk without moving the contact cone/base.
  - Base joint + base sized by `SupportSettings.baseJoint` and `SupportSettings.base` for stability.
- **Behavior**:
  - Acts as the parent/anchor for any connected branches.
  - Responsible for transferring the load of all attached branches into the build platform.
  - When a trunk is moved or resized, branches inherit the adjustment through their base attachment point.

## Branch Support
Branches are complete supports whose bases attach partway up another support instead of to the platform.

- **Role**: Extend coverage to nearby islands without consuming additional platform area. They allow one trunk to nourish several tips.
- **Structure**:
  - Full support anatomy (contact cone, shaft, joints, base joint, base) but scaled relative to the parent trunk so they remain thinner and lighter.
  - Full-featured joints: any jointing, articulation, or segmentation available to trunks must also be attachable to a branch so they can be edited identically.
  - Base attaches to a designated **branch socket** on the trunk. The base still uses `SupportSettings.base` geometry, but collision/validation ensures it conforms to the trunk surface.
  - Shaft may curve or angle sharply; multi-joint setups let branches leave the trunk, clear other geometry, then reach their target island.
- **Behavior**:
  - Considered independent supports for editing: they can be selected, transformed, or deleted without affecting the trunk tip.
  - Only behavioral difference vs. trunks: branch bases snap to another support instead of the build plate, so removing the parent trunk requires reassigning or deleting the branch.
  - Parent trunk enforces load order—if trunk is removed, dependent branches must be reassigned or removed.
  - Placement validation ensures branch bases maintain minimum clearance from other supports and that contact-cone spacing rules still apply where branch contact cones touch the model.

## Leaf Support
Leaves are ultra-light supports made entirely of contact cones that connect a branch or trunk directly to the model.

- **Role**: Provide terminal contact points when a full shaft would be overkill—ideal for fanning a branch into multiple nearby touchpoints.
- **Structure**:
  - A single contact cone where the contact face touches the model and the socket face seats into a branch or trunk socket.
  - No shaft, joints, or standalone base geometry; length is determined by the tip profile itself.
- **Behavior**:
  - Inherit transforms, selection, and deletion from their parent support.
  - Placement validation checks both the contact face (model) and socket face (receiving support) for correct overlap.
  - Removing the parent branch/trunk automatically removes dependent leaves.

## Twig Support
Twigs are contact-cone-only supports that touch the model at both faces, creating localized braces entirely within the model geometry.

- **Role**: Stabilize thin features or opposing faces of the model without anchoring to other supports or the build plate.
- **Structure**:
  - A single contact cone seated between two areas of the model: both the contact face and the socket face embed into the model surface.
  - No shafts, joints, or sockets; total length equals the contact-cone length plus penetration allowances on each side.
- **Behavior**:
  - Managed as independent supports; selecting or deleting a twig has no effect on trunks/branches.
  - Placement validation ensures both contact points maintain proper penetration and do not collide with existing supports.
  - Because they rely solely on model contact, twig feasibility depends on local wall thickness and resin tolerances.

## Brace Support
Braces are branch-style supports whose sole job is to connect two trunks (or trunk-like supports) together. Use the word “brace” only for this element to avoid confusion with standard branches or twigs.

- **Role**: Prevent tall support trees from flexing or wobbling during printing by cross-linking trunks.
- **Structure**:
  - Shares the same full-featured anatomy as a branch (contact cone, shaft, joints, base joint), but both ends attach to supports instead of the model/plate.
  - Each end acts like a branch base socket that clamps around a trunk; neither side touches the build platform or model surface.
  - Typically cylindrical/prismatic shafts for rigidity; no model-facing tip cones are used.
- **Behavior**:
  - Behaves like a double-ended branch: both bases snap to other supports, so movement of either parent repositions the brace end anchored to it.
  - Cannot host further branches or twigs; their sole function is stabilization.
  - Removal of either parent support invalidates the brace and should trigger reassignment or deletion.
- **Placement Notes**:
  - Validation must ensure adequate clearance from the model and other supports, plus enforce minimum angle/length rules to keep braces effective.
  - Because braces never anchor to the plate or model, strength calculations assume load flows from one trunk, through the brace, into the other trunk.

## Support Trees
Combining trunks and branches yields a **support tree**:

1. **Rooted trunk** provides the main connection to the build plate.
2. **Branch supports** attach to available sockets along the trunk, each terminating in its own contact cone.
3. **Multi-joint articulation** along both trunk and branches allows precise alignment to complex islands while keeping bases compact.

Designing future support types (e.g., micro branches, braces, or grid trunks) should extend this taxonomy so we always know whether a component is acting as a trunk (rooted) or as a branch (dependent on another support).
